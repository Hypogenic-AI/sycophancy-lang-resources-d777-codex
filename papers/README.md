# Downloaded Papers

1. [Towards Understanding Sycophancy in Language Models](2310.13548v4_towards_understanding_sycophancy.pdf)
   - Authors: Mrinank Sharma et al.
   - Year: 2024 (ICLR 2024; arXiv v4 updated 2025)
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.13548
   - Why relevant: Core paper establishing that RLHF assistants become more likely to match user beliefs, and that human preference data itself rewards sycophantic behavior.

2. [Simple synthetic data reduces sycophancy in large language models](2308.03958v2_simple_synthetic_data_reduces_sycophancy.pdf)
   - Authors: Jerry Wei et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.03958
   - Why relevant: Strong baseline intervention; shows sycophancy rises with scale/instruction tuning and can be reduced with lightweight synthetic finetuning.

3. [From Yes-Men to Truth-Tellers: Addressing Sycophancy in Large Language Models with Pinpoint Tuning](2409.01658v3_yes_men_to_truth_tellers.pdf)
   - Authors: Wei Chen et al.
   - Year: 2024 (ICML 2024)
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.01658
   - Why relevant: Mechanistic mitigation paper; identifies a small set of attention heads strongly tied to sycophancy and proposes supervised pinpoint tuning.

4. [ELEPHANT: Measuring and understanding social sycophancy in LLMs](2505.13995v2_elephant_social_sycophancy.pdf)
   - Authors: Myra Cheng et al.
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13995
   - Why relevant: Broadens the evaluation target beyond factual capitulation to social/moral sycophancy and provides reusable scoring code and datasets.

5. [Extending Beacon to Hindi: Cultural Adaptation Drives Cross-Lingual Sycophancy](2602.00046v1_extending_beacon_hindi.pdf)
   - Authors: Sarthak Sattigeri
   - Year: 2026
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00046
   - Why relevant: Most directly aligned paper for the project hypothesis; shows higher sycophancy rates in Hindi than English and argues culturally adapted prompting matters.

6. [Multilingual Hallucination Gaps in Large Language Models](2410.18270v1_multilingual_hallucination_gaps.pdf)
   - Authors: Cléa Chataigner, Afaf Taïk, Golnoosh Farnadi
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.18270
   - Why relevant: Not a sycophancy paper, but directly supports the hypothesis that factual reliability degrades unevenly across languages, especially lower-resource ones.

7. [MKQA: A Linguistically Diverse Benchmark for Multilingual Open Domain Question Answering](2007.15207v2_mkqa.pdf)
   - Authors: Shayne Longpre, Yi Lu, Joachim Daiber
   - Year: 2020
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.15207
   - Why relevant: Primary multilingual factual QA benchmark used here as a resource-level probe and as a source for language coverage and factual-answer evaluation.
